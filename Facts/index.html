# Here are some interesting facts about Mauritius 😏

# Mauritius is a tiny island Sitting 600 miles off the coast of Madagascar in the middle of the indian Ocean.

Img source -Mauritius Mapopen in new window

  1. It’s been Dutch, French and British
  • The Dutch first claimed (and named) Mauritius in 1638. They stayed until 1710 when France captured the territory. They re-named the island Isle de France. This didn’t last too long though, since the British invaded the island in 1810. It remained British until independence in 1968. As a result, Mauritius doesn’t have an official language, though English and French are both national languages but we do have a common language that's spoken only in Mauritius called Mauritian Créole which is a convergence of French with diferrent African Languages...
  1. Our National Bird is the DODO bird but its now extinct.

Img source -Dodo Bird Illustrationopen in new window

  • The Dodo is a lesson in extinction. Found by Dutch soldiers around 1600 on Mauritius, the Dodo became extinct less than 80 years later because of deforestation, hunting, and destruction of their nests by animals brought to the island by the Dutch.
  1. Mauritius has the highest population density in Africa.
  • With a population of 1.3 million people living in just 790 sq. miles, it has one of the world’s highest population densities at 1,634 people per sq. mile.
  1. The country is surrounded by some of the unique coral reefs.
  • Mauritius is almost entirely surrounded by lagoons protected by coral reefs, making it the world’s third-largest coral reef.
  1. Mauritius is home to an underwater waterfall
  • Just off the coast of Le Morne, you’ll find one of the world’s most spectacular illusions. Described as an ‘underwater waterfall’, it looks like the entire island is being pulled down a drain.
